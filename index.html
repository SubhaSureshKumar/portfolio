About

My journey in tech began as a ISTQB certified QA professional where I became skilled at asking the right questions, identifying edge cases, and ensuring seamless user experiences. I conducted UAT, performed root cause analysis, and managed web content of a leading mobile product company, that reached millions of users. Later, I moved into the insurance domain, testing quote & proposal and enrollment modules, where I learned that behind every smooth customer transaction is rigorous functional and regression testing.

But somewhere along the way, I realized I wanted to do more than find problems I wanted to solve them with data. During a career break, I made a deliberate choice to reskill. I immersed myself in Tableau, Power BI, Alteryx, SQL, and Python, and completed an E-Master's in Data Science & Business Analytics. I earned certifications in Tableau, and Alteryx, not just to add credentials, but to build a bridge between my testing mindset and data-driven decision-making. My analytical foundation from QA, understanding systems, identifying gaps, assessing impact translated naturally into data analysis, where I now mine data, transform them into data models that's suitable for uncovering insights that drive strategy.

Today, I combine my quality assurance rigor with analytical storytelling. I volunteer as a data analyst for a non-profit organization, contributing to social good projects that use data to create meaningful change. I'm now seeking opportunities where I can leverage both my testing expertise and newly developed analytics skills—whether through traditional roles or return-ship programs designed for professionals re-entering the workforce. I believe the best analysts aren't just technically skilled—they're curious, detail-oriented, and committed to accuracy. That's the quality mindset I bring to every dataset I touch. Outside of work I enjoy solving cognitive & logic based games, blogging, participating in community projects, hackathons and datathons.
