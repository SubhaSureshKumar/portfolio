Clinical Decision Support:

Multi-Organ Failure Monitoring in Sepsis Patients

Critical Care Intelligence Using Power BI

Project Overview

Sepsis is a life-threatening condition caused by the body's extreme response to infection, leading to tissue damage, organ failure, and death if not detected early. With mortality rates reaching 30-50% in severe cases, early identification and intervention are critical to saving lives. I Developed an interactive Power BI dashboard to monitor sepsis progression across 40,000+ patient records, identify high-risk patients, track multi-organ failure indicators, and enable clinical teams to intervene before patients reach septic shock.

Problem Statement

Sepsis affects approximately 1.7 million adults in America each year and contributes to 270,000+ deaths. Early detection is critical, as each hour of delayed treatment increases mortality risk by 4-8%. Healthcare organizations needed a comprehensive analytical tool to:

  • Identify high-risk patient demographics
  • Track progression from SIRS to septic shock
  • Monitor organ failure patterns (kidney, liver, heart, lungs)
  • Analyze ICU/SICU admission patterns and length of stay
  • Enable data-driven early intervention strategies

Data Preparation

  • Dataset: 40,336 patient records with demographic, clinical, and temporal data
  • Key Variables: Age, gender, vital signs (HR, temp, respiratory rate, WBC), organ function markers (BUN, creatinine, chloride, lactate, troponin, bilirubin), ICU/SICU status
  • Data Cleaning: Handled missing values, standardized medical units, validated clinical ranges
  • Feature Engineering: Created calculated fields for SIRS criteria, organ failure indicators, age groups, and risk stratification
  • Data Model: Star Schema design with central Patient fact table

Dashboard Design Strategy

  • Progressive Disclosure: Structured pages from overview ‚Üí SIRS ‚Üí organ-specific analysis ‚Üí septic shock
  • Clinical Workflow Alignment: Mirrored actual sepsis progression pathway for intuitive navigation
  • Interactive Filtering: Age group slicers, gender filters, sepsis label toggles for dynamic exploration
  • Color-Coded Severity: Yellow (SIRS) ‚Üí Orange (Sepsis) ‚Üí Red (Severe Sepsis) ‚Üí Dark Red (Septic Shock)

Advanced Analytics & DAX Calculations

  • Created measures for sepsis onset rate, ICU admission ratios, average length of stay by severity
  • Built risk stratification models based on age and vital signs
  • Developed organ failure scoring based on clinical thresholds (BUN/Creatinine ratio, bilirubin levels, troponin elevation, lactate accumulation)
  • Implemented time-series analysis for patient trajectory tracking

Key DAX Measures (Examples)


Sepsis Onset Rate = DIVIDE(CALCULATE(COUNT('Patient'[Patient_ID]), 'Patient'[Sepsis_Label] = 1),COUNT('Patient'[Patient_ID]),0)* 100
Average LOS by Sepsis = CALCULATE(AVERAGE('Patient'[Length_of_Stay]),'Patient'[Sepsis_Label] = 1)
Organ Failure Count = COUNTROWS(FILTER('Patient','Patient'[BCR_Ratio] > 20 || 'Patient'[Bilirubin_Direct] > 0.3 ||
 'Patient'[Troponin] > 0.04 || 'Patient'[Lactate] > 1))
